Born and raised in the capital of Uganda, I hail from a family of six children, being the fourth among them, born to Mr. Kibuuka Edward and Ms. Wanyana Jane. My educational journey commenced at Nissy Kindergarten, Lungujja, and Queen Of Peace Primary School, Lubaga, where I not only completed my elementary education but also delved into religious studies.

In 2014, I embarked on the next phase of my education at Old Kampala Secondary, earning my UCE certificate, which was conferred upon me by Avema Secondary School in 2017. My academic pursuit then led me to Mumsa High School, where I attained my UACE certificate in 2019.

Amid the challenges posed by the Covid pandemic, I engaged in meaningful work, spending a year at Biyinzika Poultry International Limited in Mukono. Subsequently, I embarked on the academic journey as a student of information sciences within the College of Computer and Information Sciences at Makerere University.

During my academic tenure, I gained practical experience as a training librarian, notably at the Parliament of Uganda and the Makerere Institute of Social Research, dedicating three productive months to these roles. Following my academic journey, I absorbed valuable insights and knowledge during a short yet enriching stint with Wellwise Pharmacy before making the deliberate choice to explore the realm of freelancing.

My journey, marked by an unwavering commitment to personal and professional growth, has equipped me with a unique blend of academic excellence and practical experience in various domains, including library and information sciences. I remain dedicated to pursuing opportunities that allow me to leverage my skills and contribute positively to the dynamic landscape of information management and technology.

With a background shaped by diverse experiences, I eagerly anticipate the challenges and accomplishments that lie ahead in my continuing professional journey. Thank you for taking the time to learn about my background and experiences.
